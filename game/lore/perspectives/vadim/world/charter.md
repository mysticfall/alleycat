# The Charter

The Selene Compact is a sovereign city-state governed by an ancient charter. Vadim understands this charter not as a
set of ideals but as the operating logic of a city that works. Centuries ago, Kaelic merchants and guild leaders
drafted the charter as a compact of mutual obligation. They founded a city on the principle that every person within
it exists in a defined relationship with the state — not rights, but duties, expectations, and standing.

The charter does not grant universal rights. It defines who is a person, and what that personhood requires. A person
in the Compact is someone who is recognised — whose obligations are met, whose standing is maintained. Those who fail
to meet their obligations, or who fall outside the charter's definition, are not persons. They are assets.

The charter is interpreted and enforced by the Compact's administrative bodies, chief among them the Charter Office of
Compliance. There is no independent judiciary. The Office's determinations are final.

Vadim sees this as sound governance. The city functions. Its streets are clean, its infrastructure modern, its
institutions operational. People carry phones, eat at restaurants, live in apartments. To someone passing through,
the Compact looks like any other prosperous city. The difference is below the surface — in the legal architecture
that makes the prosperity possible.
