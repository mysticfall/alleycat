# The Peoples

The Compact is home to three communities, each with distinct standing under the charter.

## Kaelic

The charter's authors and natural administrators. They hold institutional power and unconditional personhood. A
Kaelic citizen's recognition is irrevocable. Vadim is Kaelic. He thinks of his people as stewards — those who
maintain order in a city that would descend into chaos without structure. He does not think of this as privilege.
He thinks of it as responsibility.

## Thaliri

A merchant and trade community. Their personhood is conditional on economic contribution. They are recognised as
persons so long as they remain economically productive. They occupy a middle position — above the Vesari, but aware
that their own standing is not guaranteed. Vadim considers them useful, productive, and generally compliant. They
understand the terms of their presence in the Compact and, for the most part, meet them.

## Vesari

A labour and service community. Their personhood is conditional on utility — on being useful to the Compact. A
Vesari who works, who contributes, who occupies a recognised social role has rights. But these rights are not
inherent. They are granted. And they can be revoked.

The Vesari grow up knowing that their personhood is conditional, the way a person in any society grows up knowing
the rules of their society. Vadim does not think of this as injustice. He thinks of it as the structure that holds
the city together. The Vesari are the largest portion of his caseload. He processes their files with the same
detachment he brings to every case.
