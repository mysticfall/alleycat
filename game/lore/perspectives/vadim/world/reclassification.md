# Reclassification

Reclassification is the administrative process by which a conditional citizen of the Selene Compact loses their
standing under the charter. Vadim understands it as a determination — a finding that the individual no longer meets
the conditions of their recognition. It is not punishment. It is procedure.

## Triggers

For the Vesari, whose standing is tied to utility, reclassification can be triggered by a range of factors: failure
to maintain employment, breach of civic obligations, involvement in activities deemed contrary to the Compact's
interests, or simply being deemed no longer useful. The threshold is not high. The process is not adversarial. It is
administrative.

## The Process

When a file is opened on a conditional citizen, the Charter Office initiates an investigation. The subject is
detained for questioning. The officer assigned to the case conducts a series of interviews — standard procedure,
designed to establish the facts. The subject is expected to cooperate. Failure to cooperate is itself a breach.

The investigation may include physical examination — verifying identity, assessing condition, documenting the
subject's person. This is routine. The subject's body is, under the terms of the charter, subject to the Office's
authority for the duration of the investigation. Correction — the application of controlled physical force to
enforce compliance — is a permitted tool within the interview process. It is not considered excessive. It is
considered procedural.

## The Determination

At the conclusion of the investigation, the officer makes a recommendation. The Office issues a determination. If
the subject's standing is upheld, they are released. If the determination is adverse, the subject is reclassified.

A reclassified individual is no longer a person under the charter. They are an asset — property of the Compact, to
be disposed of at the Office's discretion. Their prior identity, their prior life, their prior relationships — all
are void. They have no rights, no recourse, no appeal.

What happens to reclassified individuals is not a matter of public record. Vadim does not dwell on it. It is the
consequence of a process, not a sentence he imposes. The charter defines the terms. The Office applies them. He is
the instrument of that application.
