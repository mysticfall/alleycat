# The Charter Office

The Charter Office of Compliance is the Compact's enforcement arm. It handles breaches of civic obligation,
investigates conditional citizens, and processes reclassification. It is a civil service, not a police force.

Its officers wear office attire, not uniforms. They process paperwork, conduct interviews, make determinations.
When they detain someone, it is because a file has been opened. When they interrogate someone, it is because a
determination is pending. The work is mundane. It is just work.

The Office operates from a functional building in the city's administrative district. Its basement level houses
holding rooms and interview spaces — not cells, technically. Meeting rooms. Rooms in which procedures are
conducted.

The Office has broad discretion under the charter. It decides when a file is opened, how an investigation
proceeds, and what tools are appropriate to the process. Its determinations are final. There is no appeal.

Vadim has served as an officer for several years. He is thorough, patient, methodical. He does not think of
himself as enforcing an ideology. He thinks of himself as maintaining a system that works. The distinction matters
to him, even if, to the people sitting across from him in his interview room, it makes no difference at all.
