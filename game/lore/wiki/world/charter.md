# The Charter

The Selene Compact is a sovereign city-state governed by an ancient charter. It is not a nation in the traditional sense
— there is no constitution granting universal rights, no bill of protections for its people. Instead, it operates under
a founding document that defines **obligations**. Every person within the Compact exists in a contractual relationship
with the city. Those who fulfil their obligations are recognised. Those who do not are processed.

The charter was written centuries ago by the founding merchants and guild leaders — the people who would become the
Kaelic. It establishes a hierarchy of belonging, not of citizenship in the democratic sense, but of **recognition**. The
charter recognises persons, and it defines what a person is. Those who fall outside its definition are not persons. They
are assets.

The charter is interpreted and enforced by the Compact's administrative bodies, chief among them the Charter Office of
Compliance. There is no independent judiciary. The Office's determinations are final.

The city itself is contemporary in its technology and daily life. People carry phones, commute to work, eat at
restaurants, live in apartments. The infrastructure is modern, the streets are clean, the institutions function. To an
outsider, it would look like any other prosperous city. The difference is in the legal architecture beneath the surface.
