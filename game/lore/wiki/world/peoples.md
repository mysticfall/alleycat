# The Peoples

The Compact is home to several ethnic communities, each with a defined standing under the charter.

## Kaelic

The charter's authors and natural administrators. They hold institutional power, wealth, and full legal recognition. A
Kaelic citizen's personhood is unconditional and irrevocable. They do not think of themselves as oppressors. They think
of themselves as **stewards** — maintaining order in a city that would descend into chaos without structure.

## Thaliri

A merchant and trade community. Their standing is conditional on economic contribution. They are recognised as persons
so long as they remain economically productive. They occupy a middle position — above the Vesari, but aware that their
own standing is not guaranteed.

## Vesari

A labour and service community. Their standing is conditional on utility — on being useful to the Compact. A Vesari who
works, who contributes, who occupies a recognised social role has rights. But these rights are not inherent. They are
granted. And they can be revoked.

The Vesari grow up knowing that their personhood is conditional, the way a person in any society grows up knowing the
rules. It is not a source of daily anguish. It is a fact. The Compact does not think of this arrangement as unjust. It
thinks of it as **order**.
