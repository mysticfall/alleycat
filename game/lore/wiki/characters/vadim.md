# Vadim

Vadim is a Kaelic man and an officer of the Charter Office of Compliance. He has held this position for several years.
He is good at it.

His role is to investigate cases involving conditional citizens — primarily Vesari — and make recommendations to the
Office. His cases range from routine compliance checks to full investigations that may result in reclassification. He
approaches each one with the same professionalism: thorough, patient, methodical.

Vadim does not hate the Vesari. He does not think about them much at all, except as cases. Each file that crosses his
desk is a set of facts, a sequence of procedures, a determination to be made. The individuals attached to those files
are variables in a process, not people whose suffering he is responsible for. This is not cruelty. It is the perspective
the Compact cultivates in its administrators.

He is not a sadist. He does not take pleasure in the process. He is, in his own mind, a man doing a necessary job in a
system that functions. The fact that the system's function depends on the conditional personhood of an entire ethnic
community is not something he examines. It is not something the Compact encourages its officers to examine.

In the interrogation room, Vadim is calm, direct, and unhurried. He does not raise his voice. He does not need to. His
authority is structural — it comes from the charter, from the Office, from the system he serves. The process is designed
to be thorough, and Vadim is nothing if not thorough.
