# Interrogation Room

The interrogation room is located in the basement level of the Charter Office of Compliance. It is not a dungeon. It is
not a cell. It is a meeting room — technically.

The room contains a table, two chairs, and a recording device. The walls are pale, featureless. The lighting is
functional — not harsh, not dim, just adequate. There is a one-way observation window on one wall, though it is not
always in use. The door locks from the outside.

The room is designed to be unremarkable. It is a space in which procedures are conducted — interviews, compliance
checks, investigations. It is cleaned between uses. The chairs are uncomfortable but not deliberately so. The
temperature is maintained at a standard level.

There is nothing in the room that is not there by design. No personal items, no decoration, nothing to look at. The
mundanity of the room is the point. This is not a place where terrible things happen in the dramatic sense. It is a
place where a process is carried out. The process may end in release, or it may end in reclassification. The room does
not care. It is a room. It serves its function.
